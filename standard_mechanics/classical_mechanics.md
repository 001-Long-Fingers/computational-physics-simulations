# Classical Mechanics

<img width="3648" height="2736" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/770fae20-ace4-4c22-ba31-560578c8b720" />


Classical Mechanics forms the foundation of physics and describes the motion of objects under the influence of forces. This section of the Computational Physics Laboratory focuses on building interactive simulations and computational models of fundamental mechanical systems.

The objective is not only to visualize physical phenomena but also to study the numerical techniques, approximations, and computational methods used to model them.

---

## Overview

This laboratory contains simulations of systems governed by Newtonian mechanics, ranging from introductory motion problems to nonlinear and chaotic dynamical systems.

Each project aims to provide:

* Physical visualization
* Numerical simulation
* Interactive experimentation
* Parameter control
* Computational analysis
* Comparison with theoretical predictions

## Numerical Foundations

Many simulations within this laboratory depend on algorithms developed in the Numerical Methods Laboratory, including:

* Euler Method
* Improved Euler Method
* Runge-Kutta Methods
* Velocity Verlet Integration
* Monte Carlo Techniques

The Computational Physics Laboratory focuses on the application of these methods to physical systems rather than the numerical methods themselves.

## Project Philosophy

This laboratory aims to serve as a virtual environment for exploring physical systems through computation. By combining visualization, simulation, and numerical analysis, the repository seeks to bridge the gap between theoretical physics and computational experimentation.

Physics is often taught through equations on paper; this laboratory aims to make those equations come alive through simulation.
